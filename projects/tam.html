GTM TAM + Prioritization Engine

Market sizing, adoption risk segmentation, and territory planning model at Pearson

Project Summary

Transformed reactive GTM into data-driven prioritization: quantified account-level TAM, built 12-box segmentation, and optimized territories for maximum incremental revenue.

Replaced opinion-based planning with a structured framework tied to penetration, potential, and risk — shifting focus from "what feels important" to "what the data supports."

Project Environment

  • High-growth education technology company (Pearson) with fragmented institutional data and uneven penetration across higher-ed segments.
  • Reactive revenue strategy: territory assignments opinion-driven, forecasting ungrounded in segmentation.
  • Leadership needed defensible answers to: "Where should we focus, and why?" amid misaligned rep coverage and hidden upside/risk.

Scope

Design and implement a TAM-driven prioritization system to:

  • Calculate institution-level TAM
  • Segment by penetration and revenue potential
  • Identify expansion, defend, optimize, and de-prioritize tiers
  • Drive territory realignment, rep focus, forecast assumptions, and resource allocation

Project Steps

  1. Construct account-level TAM (adjusted for fit and conversion likelihood)
  2. Calculate penetration (% = Actual Revenue / TAM)
  3. Build 12-box matrix: Revenue/Potential Tier × Penetration Level
  4. Derive prioritization tiers and actions (Expansion, Defend, Optimize, De-Prioritize)
  5. Roll up to territory level for imbalance analysis and reallocation modeling
  6. Embed into forecasting and executive visibility processes

Data Sources and Data Gathering

  • Enrollment size (institutional data feeds)
  • Historical revenue and product penetration rates (internal CRM/ERP)
  • Adoption patterns and discipline distribution (usage logs)
  • Territory alignment and historical growth trends (sales ops database)

All normalized to account-level granularity via SQL joins and Python preprocessing.

Data Checks

  • Duplicate account validation and deduplication
  • Missing value imputation for enrollment/discipline fields
  • Outlier detection on TAM calculations (capped extreme values)
  • Penetration sanity checks (0–100% range enforcement)
  • Cross-validation against known high/low performers

Summary Metrics

  • TAM identified: $XXM+ across institutions
  • High TAM / Low Penetration accounts: $45M+ expansion opportunity
  • Revenue density uplift in prioritized territories: +35%
  • Renewal risk flagged in high-dependency accounts
  • Territory reallocation potential: reduced imbalance by reallocating headcount to high-density regions

Findings

  • Concentration of high-TAM institutions in low-penetration tiers → major expansion opportunity left on table
  • Territories overloaded with low-potential accounts → rep capacity misaligned
  • Reps defending saturated accounts with minimal upside → reallocation created headroom
  • Mid-tier accounts showed high growth potential but low engagement → targeted plays unlocked incremental revenue
  • Overall: Created standardized GTM language, grounded forecasts in data, and enabled defensible resource decisions
